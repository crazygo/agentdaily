AI Doesn't Reduce Work—It Intensifies It

Aruna Ranganathan and Xingqi Maggie Ye from Berkeley Haas report findings from their April to December 2025 study of 200 employees. AI introduced a new rhythm in which workers managed several active threads at once: manually writing code while AI generated an alternative version, running multiple agents in parallel, or reviving long-deferred tasks because AI could 'handle them' in the background. This created cognitive load and a sense of always juggling, even as the work felt productive.
